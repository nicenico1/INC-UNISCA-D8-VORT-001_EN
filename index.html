Common Chain of Domination

The Vortigaunt established a direct link between the enslavement of the Vortigaunts and that of humans: both species were victims of the same oppressive hierarchy. The "puppets," living entities that served as intermediaries, are now endowed with consciousness since the disappearance of the main jailer, the Nihilant.

Iris Norberg noted that the Vortigaunts could serve as a "historical example" of what humans are currently living through. In an aside, Norberg suggested seeing them as a Mongol-type tribal organization, unified by the strongest and the natural order.

This directly illuminates how the subject experienced his detention: remaining motionless in a cage, in Kra'Thral terms, is to cease to exist.

I note that it is also this response that triggered the first friction between the security personnel and Dr. Shéol Caron, which is developed below.

Analysis — Shéol Caron's Question as Catalyst

Dr. Shéol Caron's question, "What interest do you have in remaining in this room imprisoned, if you can free yourself at any moment?", constituted the direct and identifiable trigger of the events that followed. In asking it, she involuntarily forced the subject to rationally confront his own situation: he had no reason to stay.

The reply "You have just made us aware, Scholar" was delivered just after a statement by Lua Tyler offering to bring the hierarchical chief. The term "Scholar" in Vortigue designates a being of knowledge or a researcher. It is likely that this awakening was the product of both interventions combined: Shéol's questions that initiated the despair, and Tyler's hollow offer that crystallized it. The Vortigaunt concluded: "In truth we have no more interest in continuing."

From that moment, the subject ceased all dialogue and began murmuring in Vortiges. Dr. Caron's reply, "And I will get over it", was the last thing he heard before tipping over.

The subject then began murmuring in Vortiges in an increasingly sustained manner, before pronouncing aloud in the Xendarian language: ⎍⋏⍙⟟⌇⒐ ⋔⟟⋏⟒ ⌰⏃⌇⏁⌰⊬ ⎐⟟ ⋔⏃⌿ ☊⟟⏁⊬. Its translation remains undetermined. The staff then began perceiving the first signs of energy accumulation.

Following the fire and partial collapse of the UNISCA laboratory, a search and recovery operation was conducted throughout the rubble. The body of the Vortigaunt designated "Kra" was not found. Post-Incident Search Results

The entire UNISCA D8 laboratory was systematically reviewed after the fire was extinguished. The findings are as follows:

Found: Blood traces of vortigaunt origin, visually confirmed by their characteristic coloration. The traces were spread across several distinct areas of the lab, consistent with the initial position of the containment pod and surrounding zones.

Two hypotheses are currently under consideration:

Hypothesis 1 — Vaporization: The extreme violence and heat of the vortesque explosion combined with the total lab fire could have completely vaporized the subject's body. A self-destruction of this magnitude could theoretically leave no solid remains. Blood traces present before the final explosion would explain what was found.

Hypothesis 2 — Teleportation: It was established during the session that certain Vortigaunts possess spatial displacement capabilities. If the subject, at the last instant, channeled his Vortessence not to fully self-destruct but to teleport — simulating an explosion while extracting himself — that would explain the absence of a body. The blood traces found could predate the event or result from partial transfer.

In the current state, the subject's status cannot officially be confirmed as "death." The case remains open.
◈
The subject had stated during the session that "dying is not something we fear" and that certain Sages of his people were capable of "traveling through worlds and dimensions". He had also, during the fake self-destruction, proven his ability to initiate and then voluntarily stop energy accumulation. These two elements lead me not to rule out the possibility that he simulated his own death. I have no certainty on this, but it would be imprudent to ignore it.

Last Words of the Subject

In his final moments, the Vortigaunt pronounced several words in rapid succession. He first accused the staff of lying about promised improvements to his conditions: "You have been promising improvements for weeks! You lie to us! You only know how to lie." He then pronounced the Xendarian term Dey'nahh — translation unknown — followed by: "Honor us as a Warrior."

His very last word spoken, screamed in the laboratory a fraction of a second before triggering the explosion, was:

"FREEDOM."

This term — in English — remains to this day without explanation as to its meaning in this context. Its origin, its target, and its precise meaning are unknown. It nonetheless constitutes the last act of conscious communication from the subject before his disappearance.

During the session, the subject had declared: "Dying is not something we fear. Especially if it is in combat... For a Kra'Thral it is almost natural."
